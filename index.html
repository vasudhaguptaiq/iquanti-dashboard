              📋 Risk Summary — May'26

              Summary:

              This report provides an integrated view of the health of key client projects, highlighting revenue risks and outlining current mitigation efforts for 2026.

              Projects with Elevated Risks

              • Aritzia-SEO (Bridge Scope): The client is evaluating bringing SEO/GEO capabilities in-house under new e-commerce leadership, creating renewal risk beyond the current bridge scope. Interest currently remains limited to a short-term technical SEO extension, with additional GEO scope potentially impacted by exploration of an internal dynamic rendering solution.
              • Brimar-PCM: The Google MCC breach intensified existing strategic and efficiency concerns, with recent optimization pivots not yet stabilizing performance amid increased competition and product mix changes. Client trust is gradually recovering, though leadership expects stronger proactive strategic guidance moving forward.
              • HSBC APAC-SEO: Client feedback on the engagement remains positive; however, implementation challenges within the Philippines team continue to create renewal risk as the client focuses on internal priorities before revisiting longer-term engagement discussions.
              • Sunrise Brands – Donald Pliner-PCM: The client has raised quality concerns related to resource and skill gaps, particularly around retail industry and business context understanding. Interim resource support remains in place while hiring efforts continue, alongside active client communication and development of a more detailed action plan.
              • Kapitus PCM: Performance across April and May continues to remain challenging despite SME involvement and optimization strategy adjustments. The team is evaluating the need for dedicated SME support, as progress in stabilizing performance has remained limited.

              Positive Developments

              • UOB-TH-SEO: Geo-tracking gaps have been resolved through the implementation of PEEC.AI, providing the client with meaningful and reliable geo-level insights.
              • Bread Financial: The engagement has stabilized following earlier breach-related concerns, with overall conditions continuing to improve.

              Areas Requiring Continued Focus

              • Amex-MCG-Analytics: The team has narrowed the root cause impacting data consistency across the user journey. Additional analytics support is being engaged to strengthen planning visibility while deeper validation of tagging setup and event flow continues toward a long-term remediation path.
              • Amex D&D: Resource coverage is being strengthened through DevOps backup support, expanded on-call alerting, and extended developer availability. A formalized escalation process is still being developed as a preventive measure.
              • Discover Credit Cards-SEO: Agency consolidation discussions at CapOne have introduced uncertainty around future scope. The situation is being monitored closely as engagement structure decisions evolve.

              Other Key Observations

              Active risks this period continue to be weighted toward the Delivery category, with several areas potentially addressable through bridging SME gaps, strengthening non-BFSI business skill sets, and resourcing improvements, rather than external market or client-driven factors.
